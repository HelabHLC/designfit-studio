# CMT Spectral Data — Permission and Attribution Record

## Source

- **System:** Color Mixing Tools (CMT)
- **Relevant application:** rs2color
- **Institution:** University of Brescia
- **Developer:** Prof. Zsolt M. Kovacs-Vajna

## Permission

On 19 July 2026, Prof. Zsolt M. Kovacs-Vajna confirmed in direct correspondence that data made available through CMT may be used for research and educational purposes without an additional licence, provided that the origin of the data is clearly stated.

When only part of a dataset is used, the specific source and relevant subset must be identified clearly in the reference.

## Conditions recorded by ARBE

Use of CMT data within ARBE is subject to the following conditions:

1. The data source must be stated clearly.
2. The specific database or dataset subset must be identified where known.
3. CMT data must not be presented as ARBE measurement data.
4. Provenance must be retained in derived results, diagrams and reports.
5. Scientific publications must assess whether the contribution of the data and its author warrants acknowledgement or co-authorship.
6. Use is currently limited to research and educational purposes.
7. Commercial use, sublicensing and third-party redistribution are not inferred from this permission and require separate review.

## Evidence

Permission was provided by direct email correspondence dated 19 July 2026.

The original correspondence is retained in the private project records and is not published here in order to protect personal contact details and correspondence privacy.

## ARBE classification

- **Permission type:** Direct written permission
- **Permitted purpose:** Research and education
- **Attribution required:** Yes
- **Additional licence required:** No, within the stated scope
- **Redistribution of original datasets:** Not explicitly confirmed
- **Commercial use:** Not confirmed
- **Evidence holder:** Project owner

## Implementation requirement

Any CMT-derived or CMT-sourced dataset used by ARBE should retain machine-readable provenance metadata, including the original database name, identified data author where available, permission basis, permission date, attribution requirement and redistribution status.

This record documents permission and scope only. It does not transfer ownership of any source dataset and does not classify CMT data as open data.
